Liquid Layout

Mmm! Drink it in. It always goes down smooth.

When most people think about web design (if they think about web design at all), they often think of it in terms of aesthetic appeal. A well-designed site is one that looks good to the eye and feels good to navigate. (Unfortunately the senses of smell and taste don't come into play for websites. Although IGN should start looking in to that. Perhaps I can head up that team this summer. Imagine scratch-n-sniff sites!? I can!). Whether it's the sexy curves of a PS3 or 360 controller (think I need a girlfriend) or the texture of their thumb sticks, the aesthetic dimension of product design is a sure attention-getter.

For any kind of product or service, it's the little things that count. Having a button click when you push it down doesn't seem like much, but when that click makes the difference between killing and getting killed, it matters a great deal (see: rage quit). Even if you never realized that the design of that button was causing you trouble, how would you feel about a controller that you were able to use successfully only part of the time? How would you feel about Sony or Microsoft? Would you buy another product from them in the future? Probably not. Thus, for the want of a button that works, a customer is lost.. But wait.. It's Call of Duty, they will be back tomorrow!

Point being that user experience plays a huge role in the success or demise of a product or site and the backbone of said UX lies in the layout. Liquid layouts are layouts that are based on percentages of the current browser window's size. They flex with the size of the window, even if the viewer changes their browser size as they're viewing the site (go ahead and try it right now). Liquid width layouts allow a very efficient use of the space provided by any given Web browser window or screen resolution. The more people who can cleanly and efficiently view your site the better. This liquid site I created is based on the 960 grid system framework as well which is set to be a maximum of 960px. This layout can be viewed perfectly on the most common screen resolution 1024x768. Add that to the fact that I'm running a liquid layout and you have a match made in Hyrule!

Ping Pong

I'm-a-Wario.. I'm-a-gonna-win!!

I was never really great at the "guess the jelly beans" game back in grade school (nor the game of ping pong for that matter). I always seemed to overshoot by a mile Rainbow Road lap. Perhaps thats a sign of an upbeat and optimistic person.. Or maybe its just simply a sign of someone who has no idea how to guess jelly beans. Being this seems a lot like a cognitive ability test question and open to interpretation I should probably go with the former, but lets be honest, your guess is as good as mine as they say.

When it comes to guessing the number of ping pong balls in an average size school bus the stage gets a little bigger and the lights a little brighter. No longer am I guessing against little Jimmy and Johnny to win the jar of jelly beans and 10 extra minutes of recess. I'm guessing against some of the brightest minds in the field of computers all while trying to secure a job at a place I have dreamed of working at (Imagine Games Network) since my days of using IGN.com to master the Rainbow Road jump. I may be older and wiser (questionable) now, but without a Master's degree in mathematics or physics I feel the premise remains the same as it did in my jelly bean days. Just give it my best guess!

To be honest, the instinct is there to search google for the answer. And I can guarantee some people who submitted this application did just that. I guess these are the times we live in. You can find the answer to just about any question on the web. Then again, I'd probably wind up finding the number of ping pong balls you can fit anywhere but a school bus!

I vividly remember back in the day, watching the kids who studied the giant glass bowl intensely, trying to count every last jelly bean. That was never really my style, I played it cool (relatively) and threw out a number. Plus, it seemed as though those kids always wound up being the furthest off anyway. If there is one thing I have learned since my jelly bean days, its you cant sweat the small things. Looking through a glass bowl or the window of a school bus is only going to get you so far and help you so much in these scenarios. The best thing I can do is make an educated guess and hope for the best!

On that note, doing a google search I found the standard ping pong ball has a diameter of about 1.58 inches and the average school bus is built with inside dimensions around 35 feet long by 7.5 feet wide by 6.5 feet high. My next move is to take those numbers and convert them into a similar metric to make all my equations easier. Centimeters will work just fine, so with that the School bus is 1066.8 cm x 228.6 cm x 198.1 cm, giving it a total volume of 48,310,742.1 cm. I will place the ping pong ball into an imaginary square with dimensions of 4 cm x 4 cm x 4 cm (this is where that Master's degree would come in handy), giving it a total volume of 64 cm. Lastly I am going to perform a simple equation to find out how many ping pong balls (squares) would fit into a school bus by dividing the volume of the bus by the volume of the ball (square). The answer I return is about 754,855. So in turn, in my estimation 754,855 ping pong balls would fit into an average sized school bus. Just as I did when I was a kid I'll go with my gut and throw that number out there.. Then again, my gut is also very hungry!
